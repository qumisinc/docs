## Finding Team Members

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check Slack">
    Use Slack's people directory to find team members and their contact information.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse by Team">
    Each Slack channel has a member list showing who's part of that team.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask in #general">
    If you can't find someone, ask in #general and someone will point you in the right direction.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Contact Guidelines

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="When to use Slack" icon="messages">
    - Quick questions and discussions
    - Team coordination
    - Non-urgent requests
    - General announcements
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="When to use email" icon="envelope">
    - External communications
    - Formal requests or approvals
    - Sensitive or confidential matters
    - Documentation that needs to be preserved
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="When to schedule a meeting" icon="calendar">
    - Complex discussions requiring back-and-forth
    - Decision-making sessions
    - Project kickoffs and retrospectives
    - Performance conversations
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
  **Pro tip:** Add your working hours and timezone to your Slack profile so team members know the best time to reach you.
</Tip>
